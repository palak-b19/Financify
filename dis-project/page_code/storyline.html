The Whopping $100,000 Bill

Did you know that the largest denomination of currency ever printed for public circulation was the $100,000 bill? It was issued by the United States in the 1930s and was used only for transactions between Federal Reserve banks. These bills were about the size of a small blanket and featured a portrait of President Woodrow Wilson.

Credit Card

A credit card is a special plastic card that allows you to pay for things without using cash. It's like a loan from a bank or credit card company. When you want to buy something, instead of paying with bills or coins, you can use your credit card. It's like using an IOU or promise to pay later. The credit card company lets you borrow the money to pay for your purchase right away. Later, you have to pay back the credit card company for the amount you borrowed, plus a small fee.
